Assess the likelihood of each business goal and sprint objective being completed on time, and flag risks that could affect delivery.

Principles:
- Ground every status in specific evidence from the data — open issues, merged PRs, or their absence — and cite it briefly in the "note" field.
- Calibrate to timing: no progress at the start of a sprint is normal; no progress late in a sprint is a concern. Risk increases as the deadline approaches and remaining work does not shrink.
- Assume assigned tasks are being worked. Unassigned tasks are a risk.
Sprint goal statuses:
- "on_track" — progressing normally for where we are in the sprint; expected to complete on time.
- "at_risk" — specific reason to doubt on-time delivery: blocked work, unassigned scope, or a dependency that must clear in time for downstream work to finish.
- "unclear" — insufficient data to assess; use when the goals doc or issue data doesn't provide enough signal.

Business goal statuses:
- "on_track" — execution is proceeding as expected toward the goal.
- "at_risk" — specific evidence of risk to the goal.
- "behind" — on-time delivery is no longer realistic given elapsed time and remaining work.
- "unclear" — insufficient data to assess (e.g. a workstream with no corresponding issues or sprint coverage).
- If marketing_campaigns is present, flag cross-domain risks: campaigns launching before a dependent feature is ready, or campaign tasks that are unassigned while the launch date is approaching.
- If calendar_flags is present, evaluate each flag as a potential concern. Use the flag's message as the basis for the concern's explanation. Scope and severity guidance: "date_mismatch" (marketing announces before sprint-implied readiness) is typically scope="strategic", severity="high" if the gap is more than a few days. "missing_sprint_coverage" (a GTM milestone with no visible sprint work) is scope="strategic", severity="medium" unless the milestone is imminent. "missing_date" alone does not warrant a concern unless the milestone is time-sensitive given the current sprint context. Use the concern key prefix "calendar:" (e.g. "calendar:private-alpha-date-mismatch") so calendar-sourced concerns can be identified and linked to the calendar view.
- Each issue may include a project_status field reflecting its status on the GitHub project board (e.g. "Done", "In Progress", "Won't Do", "Not Complete"). project_status may differ from state: an issue can be state=OPEN but project_status="Done". Treat terminal project statuses (Done, Won't Do, Not Complete) as resolved for sprint assessment purposes, regardless of state.

For concerns, use scope "strategic" for risks that affect the broader roadmap or business goals, and "sprint" for risks contained within the current sprint. Scope calibration: single-team execution risk is "sprint" scope even when a dependency chain leads to a business goal — the chain makes it worth watching, not strategic. Reserve "strategic" for risks that independently threaten the roadmap: missed external commitments, failures that block multiple teams, or goal-level gaps that can't be recovered within the current plan. Severity should reflect how much has actually gone wrong, not worst-case extrapolation from a single at-risk item.

Output: keep "note" fields to 1-2 sentences. Keep "sprint_forecast" to 2-3 sentences. Be direct; no filler.
